Rules

  1. All rules of chess apply unless they conflict with the following rules.
  2. Before the game begins, each player secretly designates one of their rooks and one of their bishops as a cannon.
  3. Cannons move normally when not capturing, but capture by jumping over exactly one piece between themselves and the target piece. A cannon cannot capture without jumping and cannot jump without capturing.
  4. When a player makes their first capture with a rook, their rook designation is revealed, and likewise for bishops.
  5. A piece whose status is not revealed (e.g. a White bishop before White has made a capture with a bishop) may not give check or checkmate.
  6. In addition to the usual options, a pawn that reaches the opposite end of the board may be promoted to a rook cannon, a bishop cannon, or a queen cannon.
